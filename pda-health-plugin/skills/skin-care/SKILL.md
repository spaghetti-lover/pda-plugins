# Skin Care Routine

A practical skincare routine, from foundational daily steps to advanced active
treatments. Don't recommend every step at once — match the routine to the user's
actual skin type and concerns, and introduce actives gradually.

## Foundational Steps (do these daily)

1. **Cleanse.** The most important step. Wash your face 2x/day (morning and
   night) to remove excess oil (sebum) and dirt.
2. **Moisturize.** Maintains the skin's moisture barrier.
3. **Sunscreen.** Protects against UV and environmental damage. Apply every
   morning, even indoors or on cloudy days. (Foundational, but only needed in the
   AM routine.)

A complete beginner routine is just: **Cleanse → Moisturize → Sunscreen (AM)**.

## Advanced Steps (Treatment)

Add these only when you have specific concerns — acne, dark spots, melasma, signs
of aging. You do **not** need to use all of them at once.

### Hydration

Use a **toner** or **hyaluronic acid (HA)** for instant water-based hydration.
HA draws moisture into the skin and pairs well before applying moisturizer.

### Treatment Actives

The treatment step is where specific skin problems get solved. Choose actives
based on your concern:

| Active                         | What it does                                                        | Best for                  |
| ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| **BHA** (Beta Hydroxy Acid)    | Chemical exfoliant; oil-soluble, penetrates deep into pores         | Acne-prone, oily skin     |
| **AHA** (Alpha Hydroxy Acid)   | Chemical exfoliant on the skin's surface                            | Dull, rough, uneven tone  |
| **Retinol / Retinoid** (Vit A) | Speeds up skin renewal; smooths and firms                           | Aging, texture, acne      |
| **Niacinamide** (Vit B3)       | Controls oil, reduces redness, boosts ceramide production (barrier) | Oily, sensitive, redness  |
| **Vitamin C**                  | Brightens skin, fades dark spots                                    | Dullness, hyperpigment    |

## Important Notes

- **Don't pile on every active at once.** Introduce one at a time and watch how
  your skin reacts — combining too many (e.g. retinol + multiple acids) can
  irritate or damage the barrier.
- **Choose based on real need.** Pick products for your actual skin type and
  current condition, not because an ingredient is trendy.
- **Pair strong actives with sunscreen.** Exfoliating acids, retinoids, and
  vitamin C increase sun sensitivity, so daily sunscreen becomes essential.

## Quick Reference

| Step        | Routine | Frequency / Rule                          |
| ----------- | ------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Cleanse     | AM + PM | 2x/day                                    |
| Hydrate     | AM + PM | Toner / HA as needed                      |
| Treatment   | PM      | Start with one active, build up slowly    |
| Moisturize  | AM + PM | Always                                     |
| Sunscreen   | AM      | Every morning, reapply if outdoors        |
