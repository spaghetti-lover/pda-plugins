# Hair Care Routine

A practical, evidence-informed routine for keeping hair healthy. Apply the steps
that match the user's situation rather than dumping all of them at once — ask
about their hair type, environment, and whether their hair is bleached/colored.

## 1. Cleansing (Shampoo & Conditioner)

- **Don't wash daily.** Wash every other day or about 2x/week. Adjust to your
  environment — dustier, more polluted surroundings may need slightly more
  frequent washing.
- **Use conditioner in moderation.** At most 3x/week. Overusing conditioner can
  clog and suffocate the scalp.
- **Keep accessories clean.** Regularly clean helmets and hats — dirty headwear
  is a common reason hair gets greasy and flat.

## 2. After Showering (Drying)

- **Dry gently.** Hair is weakest when wet, so avoid vigorously rubbing or
  scrubbing your scalp. Pat dry softly with a towel or wrap it up loosely.
- **Always pre-style before heat.** Apply a hair oil or heat-protectant product
  before blow-drying to shield hair from high temperatures.
- **Use a comb while drying.** Combing as you dry helps hair set smoothly. Clean
  or replace the comb roughly once a month.

## 3. Nourishing & Styling

- **Hair oil for moisture.** Lightweight hydrating oils (e.g. Forte Hydrating
  Oil, 1821 Heavy Oil) add moisture — especially helpful for bleached or colored
  hair.
- **Heavy oils only before washing.** Heavy oils can weigh hair down and make it
  greasy, so apply them only before a wash, not as a leave-in styling product.

## 4. Maintenance Habits

- **Keep your sleeping environment clean.** Wash bed sheets and pillowcases
  regularly so dust and oil don't transfer back onto your scalp and hair.
- **Build a fixed routine.** The biggest factor is consistency — follow the plan
  steadily over the long term to genuinely improve hair health.

## Quick Reference
